About Me

I am a first‑year PhD student in Computer Science at CU Boulder. My research interests lie at the intersection of systems for machine learning, LLM inference optimisation (e.g. KV‑cache sharing and prefix sharing), and the science of science (bibliometrics, AI‑for‑science pipelines and large‑scale data analysis). I enjoy designing efficient multi‑agent LLM systems, long‑context optimisations and data pipelines based on large academic datasets.

Research

Prefix‑Sharing for Multi‑Agent LLM Systems

Systems‑level acceleration for multi‑agent inference: compared baseline versus prefix‑sharing inference, designed templates to isolate shared prefix behaviour and evaluated on long prompts. Framework: PyTorch, vLLM and a custom KV‑cache tracer.

Tiny vLLM Implementation

A lightweight imitation of vLLM to understand LLM inference engine design. Implemented KV‑cache reuse across tokens, built a minimal paged‑attention‑like mechanism and benchmarked improvements over the Hugging Face baseline.

500k‑Paper OpenAlex AI Bibliometrics Dataset

Collected more than 500k AI papers (2010–2023), built a PySpark pipeline for citation analysis, reproduced top‑10 % LOESS trending across global regions and studied within‑group citation patterns.

SemEval MT‑RAG System

Developed a multi‑task retrieval‑augmented generation system with BM25 and BGE embeddings, long‑context reasoning and structured agent workflows.
