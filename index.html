About Me

I am a first‑year PhD student in Computer Science at University of Colorado Boulder. My research interests lie at the intersection of systems for machine learning (LLM inference optimisation), and the science of science (bibliometrics, AI‑for‑science pipelines and large‑scale data analysis). I enjoy designing efficient multi‑agent LLM systems, long‑context optimisations and data pipelines based on large academic datasets.

Research

Prefix‑Sharing for Multi‑Agent LLM Systems

Systems‑level acceleration for multi‑agent inference: compared baseline versus prefix‑sharing inference, designed templates to isolate shared prefix behaviour and evaluated on long prompts. Framework: PyTorch, vLLM and a custom KV‑cache tracer.

[Upcoming] Global AI Contribution&Competition Landscape

analyzed 536,350 papers from 2010 to 2023 from the field of AI and compared productivity, impact, novelty, and citation patterns across the U.S., Europe, and China.

SemEval MT‑RAG System

Developed a multi‑task retrieval‑augmented generation system with BGE embeddings, long‑context reasoning and structured agent workflows.
